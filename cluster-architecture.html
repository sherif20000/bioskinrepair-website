Section 01
Google SEO Ranking Factors
Weighted by impact for a health/skincare niche content site. Based on Google's Quality Rater Guidelines, Search Central documentation, and third-party correlation studies (Ahrefs, Semrush, Backlinko).
Topical Authority / Cluster Depth
Critical
Sites that cover a topic comprehensively outrank single-page specialists. Hub + spoke architecture is the primary lever for authority building. Target: 10+ pages per cluster before expecting competitive rankings.
E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust)
Critical
Health content is classified as "Your Money or Your Life" (YMYL). Google applies heightened E-E-A-T scrutiny. Requires: author bio with credentials, About page, editorial policy, and cited sources on every article.
Content Quality & Depth
Critical
Word count matters only insofar as it correlates with completeness. Competitor gap analysis: cover every subtopic a top-10 result covers, plus at least one angle they don't. Target 1,200–2,500 words for spoke articles.
Core Web Vitals (LCP, CLS, FID)
High
Page experience is a confirmed ranking signal. LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, FID < 100ms. Static HTML site has inherent advantage — avoid lazy-loading hero images and ensure fonts are preloaded.
Internal Linking Structure
High
PageRank flows through internal links. Hub pages must link to all spokes. Spokes must link back to hub and cross-link to related spokes. Target 3–5 contextual internal links per article, using keyword-rich anchor text.
Schema Markup (Structured Data)
High
Rich snippets (FAQs, breadcrumbs, review stars) improve CTR by 15–30%. Article schema signals recency. FAQPage schema directly feeds AI Overviews. Review/Product schema enables star ratings in SERPs.
Backlink Authority
High
Domain Rating (DR) is the primary correlation with top-10 rankings. Start link building at Month 3 when content library is sufficient. Target: skincare blogs, Reddit wiki citations, Quora answers linking to articles.
Search Intent Match
High
Every page must satisfy the dominant intent: informational (what/how/why), commercial (best/review/vs), or navigational. Wrong format for the intent = bounce rate spike = ranking drop. Verify intent before writing.
Keyword Optimization (On-Page)
Medium
Primary keyword in: title tag, H1, first 100 words, one H2, meta description, URL slug. Secondary keywords in H2s and naturally in body. Avoid keyword stuffing — TF-IDF, not density.
URL Structure & Sitemap
Medium
Clean URLs (clean URLs already configured via Vercel). Sitemap submitted to Google Search Console. All new pages must be added to sitemap.xml within 24h of publication. Request indexing via GSC for each new URL.
Image Optimisation & Alt Text
Medium
CSS product tiles eliminate broken image risk. For any future real photography: WebP format, descriptive alt text with keyword, max 150KB per image. Google Image Search is a secondary traffic source for skincare.
Content Freshness
Medium
Update <lastmod> in sitemap.xml when articles are revised. For product reviews, add a "Last updated" dateline. High-competition queries ("best ceramide moisturizer") favor recently updated content.
Section 02
AI Overview (AIO) Ranking Factors
Google's AI Overviews pull from pages that demonstrate these specific patterns. Different from traditional SEO — prioritises direct answers and structured formatting over keyword density.
⚡
Direct Answer in First 100 Words
Every article must answer the primary question in the opening paragraph — before any preamble, context, or backstory. AI Overviews extract this opening answer verbatim.
📋
Structured Formatting (Lists, Tables, H2s)
AI models prefer structured content. Use numbered lists for steps, bullet points for symptoms, tables for comparisons, and H2s that mirror question phrasing ("How long does X take?").
🔬
Definitional Clarity
Define technical terms on first use. "Transepidermal water loss (TEWL) is the rate at which water evaporates through the skin." This entity anchoring is a primary AIO selection signal.
❓
FAQPage Schema
Add 3–5 Q&A pairs in FAQPage JSON-LD to every article. These feed directly into AI Overview sourcing. Questions must match "People Also Ask" patterns for the primary keyword.
🔗
Entity Co-occurrence
Mention related entities naturally: brand names (CeraVe, La Roche-Posay), ingredients (ceramides, niacinamide), conditions (eczema, rosacea). Entity density signals topical expertise to AI crawlers.
📚
Citation of Primary Sources
Reference journal studies, dermatological guidelines, or clinical data. AI models treat cited sources as authority signals. Format: "A 2023 study published in the Journal of Investigative Dermatology found..."
🔄
Consistent E-E-A-T Signals
Author byline with credentials on every article. About page with editorial team. Editorial policy linked from footer. AI models weight sourced, attributed content higher than anonymous editorial.
📊
Specific Numbers & Data
AI Overviews prefer content with specific claims: "Ceramides make up 50% of the lipid matrix" over "ceramides are an important part of skin." Data specificity is a strong AIO selection signal.
Section 03
Image Strategy Per Content Type
Consistent approach across all 79 pages — no Unsplash stock photos. All visuals serve the editorial credibility of the site.
Product Reviews
Score card + Related cards
Score card: CSS brand tile (gradient bg, brand name, product name, category pill) — 200px height

Related cards: CSS brand tile — 140px height, warm gradient

In-article: No product images. Instead: ingredient analysis tables, score bar charts (CSS), comparison tables

Why: CSS tiles are always "live," never 404, load in <1ms, and look more editorial than stock photos
Hub / Cluster Pages
Category landing pages
Hero: No image. Large typographic hero with eyebrow + H1 + subtitle (current approach — keep)

Card grid: CSS category icon tiles (icon + category name on brand gradient bg)

In-content: Diagrams and charts generated in CSS or SVG — no external image dependencies

Why: Hub pages should load instantly. Images add latency without adding trust.
Educational Articles
Condition guides, ingredient pages
Hero: No image. Article hero with breadcrumb + H1 + byline (current approach — keep)

Diagrams: Simple SVG or CSS illustrations for anatomical concepts (skin layers, barrier function)

Tables: Ingredient comparison tables styled in HTML — these rank in image search too

Why: Health content earns trust through clarity, not photography
Comparison / Versus Articles
CeraVe vs Cetaphil, etc.
Hero: Side-by-side CSS product tile — two brand tiles with "VS" divider in center

Comparison table: Full-width HTML table with score bars per criterion

Winner callout: Green-highlighted verdict box at top (below the fold for SEO, above for UX)

Why: Comparison intent is commercial — the winner callout converts to affiliate clicks
Buying Guide / Best Of
Best ceramide moisturizer, etc.
Hero: Category-branded CSS tile with "Editor's Picks" badge

Product cards: CSS brand tiles (consistent with review pages) + score + 1-sentence verdict

Quick-pick table: Condensed table at top ("Best Overall," "Best Budget," "Best for Eczema")

Why: Buying guides have highest affiliate click-through — product visual must render reliably
Glossary Pages
What is TEWL, humectant, etc.
Hero: Minimal. Term + pronunciation guide + one-sentence definition

Visual: Simple CSS definition card (term in Playfair Display, etymology, related terms)

No images needed — these pages are AIO-optimised for definitional queries

Why: AI Overviews pull from the definition, not the images. Clarity beats aesthetics here.

Section 05
Internal Linking Rules
Every page must follow these linking rules. Anchor text should be keyword-rich and descriptive — never "click here" or "read more."
Condition Pages (eczema, rosacea…) → link to
  • Relevant product review pages ("CeraVe for eczema — see our review")
  • Routine hub for that condition
  • Ingredient pages for condition-specific actives
  • Skin Barrier 101 hub ("understand the underlying mechanism")
Ingredient Pages → link to
  • Products containing that ingredient (review pages)
  • Buying guides featuring that ingredient class
  • Condition pages where that ingredient helps
  • Comparison articles (niacinamide vs HA)
Review Pages → link to
  • 2–3 related product reviews (at bottom "Related Reviews")
  • Buying guide that features this product
  • Ingredient pages for key actives in the formula
  • Condition page most relevant to the product's use case
Buying Guides → link to
  • Individual full review for every product listed
  • Relevant condition pages ("best moisturiser for eczema" → /eczema)
  • Ingredient hub for the key ingredient class covered
  • Routine pages for use-case context
Comparison Articles → link to
  • Full individual review for each product compared
  • Ingredient pages for the primary active in each product
  • Buying guide for the category
Glossary Pages → link to
  • Ingredient pages using that ingredient class
  • Skin Barrier 101 hub for mechanism context
  • Related glossary terms (humectant → occlusive → emollient)

Schema Priority Order

Every page: Article + BreadcrumbList. Add FAQPage to any page with Q&A sections. Reviews get Review + Product schema. Buying guides get ItemList schema. Glossary pages get DefinedTerm schema. Condition guides add MedicalCondition where clinically appropriate.

Sitemap Update Rule

Add every new page to sitemap.xml within 24h of publishing. Update <lastmod> when revising existing articles. Submit updated sitemap URL in Google Search Console after every batch of 5+ new pages. Request manual indexing for high-priority pages.

E-E-A-T Checklist (per article)

Author byline present · At least 1 citation to a study or clinical source · Dermatologist disclaimer on condition pages · "Last updated" date visible · About page linked from footer · Editorial policy page (build Month 2)

AIO Checklist (per article)

Direct answer in first 100 words · H2s phrased as questions · FAQPage schema with 3–5 PAA-aligned Q&As · Technical terms defined on first use · Specific numbers/percentages/timeframes cited · No AI-sounding preamble
